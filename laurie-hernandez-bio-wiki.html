Laurie Hernandez Bio (Wiki)

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Wednesday, August 7, 2024

Who is Laurie Hernandez?

Laurie Hernandez is an American gymnast.

She is best known for competing as a member of the U.S. women’s gymnastics team at the 2016 Summer Olympics.

Moreover, she won gold in the team event and silver on the balance beam.

She is the youngest member of the U.S. women’s gymnastics team, nicknamed “The Final Five”. She was athletic from a very young age.

How many siblings does Laurie Hernandez have? Bio, Age, Ethnicity

Laurie Hernandez was born on 9th June 2000.

Her birthplace is Old Bridge Township, New Jersey. Being the youngest gymnast, she had her training in Morganville, New Jersey.

She was born to Wanda Hernandez and Anthony Hernandez. Laurie has a sister and a brother whose name is Jelysa and Marcus respectively.

She is of Puerto Rican and Hispanic ethnicity.

Details about her academics are not disclosed.

Laurie Hernandez: Career, Salary, and Net Worth

Regarding her junior career, Laurie Hernandez’s career first started in 2012 at the U.S. Classic, where she was considered 11th in the junior division when she was 12 years old.

In 2013, Laurie first meets WOGA Classic, where she was announced as a second in the all-around.

In the time of June, she placed first on floor exercise, second in the all-around behind Ariana Agrapides, and third on balance beam and vault.

At the start of July; Hernandez was joined by the U.S. Junior National Team. After that, she went to Chicago for the U.S. Classic.

This is the place where she placed sixth all-around and won the floor exercise title.

In September, Hernandez was the one who stood for the representation of the U.S. at the Junior Japan International in Yokohama. She won the bronze medal in the all-around.

Hernandez represented herself in her senior career initially in 2016.

In March, she competed at the City of Jesolo Trophy in Jesolo, Italy where she earned the bronze medal all-around with a score of 58.550.

Furthermore, she also won many other silver and gold medals. Similarly, in April, Hernandez competed at the Pacific Rim Gymnastics.

In June, she had a competition at the U.S. Classic on bars only where she scored 15.400.

On recent years, Laurie was chosen to be one of the participants of the Season 23 cast of Dancing with the Stars, partnering with Val Chmerkovskiy.

In November 2016, they both together won the competition by beating the Canadian race car driver James Hinchcliffe and his partner, Sharna Burgess.

In the fall of 2021, Laurie participated in Simone Biles’s Gold Over America Tour.
