Lamar Odom is an American professional basketball player who was a member of the Los Angeles Lakers in the National Basketball Association (NBA) and won championships in 2009 and 2010. He has also been a part of the United States national team, Los Angeles Clippers, Dallas Mavericks, New York Knicks, and Laboral Kutxa Baskonia.

Education

Lamar first attended Christ The King Regional High School in Middle Village, Queens. However, because of poor grades, he left before his senior year and moved to Redemption Christian Academy in New York. He also attended St.Thomas Aquinas High School in New Britain, Connecticut.

After he finished high school, Lamar continued his education at the University of Nevada at Las Vegas from where he was released before the start of the season after a controversial report published by Sports Illustrated for his unexpected high score in the ACT test. Lamar was found guilty by the NCAA inquiry for receiving $5,600 from David Chapman which was against the rules of NCAA college basketball. This forced Lamar to sit out of the whole 1997-1998 season despite he already joined the University of Rhode Island.

    Lamar Odom Facts

  • After the death of his mother Cathy, Lamar was raised by his maternal grandmother Mildred Mercer.
  • He was chosen for the Parade Player of the Year in 1997.
  • Lamar wanted to join the NBA draft once he completed his high school studies. However, after a conversation with Kobe Bryant, Odom decided that he was not NBA ready and so, he enrolled himself into the University of Nevada at Las Vegas.
  • In 1999, Odom confirmed that he would enter the 1999 NBA Draft after his freshman year at college. However, a little bit later, Lamar wanted to change his decision and return to Rhode Island which did not happen because he had already registered with an agent and it was too late to withdraw from the draft.
  • In the 1999 NBA Draft, Odom was chosen as the 4th overall pick by the Los Angeles Clippers.
  • During the 2000-2001 season in Clippers, Odom averaged 17.2 points per match.
  • Lamar shifted to Miami Heat for the 2003-2004 NBA season.
  • After the 2003-2004 season, Odom was sent to Los Angeles Lakers after a trade deal between the Lakers and Miami Heat which included Lamar himself, Caron Butler, and Brian Grant in exchange for Shaquille O’Neal.
  • Lamar started in the TV show Keeping Up with the Kardashians in 2009, featured as a supporting cast member on the entertainment television E! He also performed in the TV series Khloe & Lamar which started on April 10, 2011. However, after only two seasons, the show was stopped.
  • He appeared in the 2011 comedy film, Jack and Jill, starring Adam Sandler, Katie Holmes, and Al Pacino.
  • On December 11, 2011, Odom was sent to Dallas Mavericks in exchange for the first-round pick and an 8.9 million dollar trade exception. During that time, NBA’s chairman David Stern disapproved of the potential trade deal between Los Angeles Lakers, Houston Rockets, and New Orleans which included Chris Paul, Kevin Martin, Luis Scola, Pau Gasol, and Goran Dragic.
  • On March 2, 2012, Odom was sent to the Texas Legends in the NBA Development League. Eventually, only a day after, the Mavericks returned him to their active roster.
  • Odom joined the Los Angeles Clippers on June 29, 2012, after a four-team trade.
  • On August 30, 2013, Lamar was charged by the police for driving under the influence (DUI).
  • On February 18, 2014, Odom shifted to the Spanish team Laboral Kutxa Baskonia with which he signed a two-month contract with a possibility of an extension. However, he did appear in only two games for the Spanish side because of a back injury that forced him to return to the United States for a full recovery.
  • In the middle of the 2013-2014 NBA season, Odom was penned by the New York Knicks. Eventually, on July 11, 2014, Lamar got fired.
  • He is the owner of Rich Soil Entertainment, a music and movie production company.
  • On October 13, 2015, Odom was found unconscious in a brothel in Crystal, Nevada, and was immediately sent to a rehabilitation hospital in Las Vegas. Lamar remained in the coma for few days and experienced several strokes and kidney failure. Eventually, his recovery process went well. In May 2021, during an interview on Good Morning America, he revealed that he was using ketamine to remain sober.
  • In 2015, Lamar was the most searched personality on Google Search.
  • In his memoir Darkness to Light, Lamar revealed that he had slept with more than 2,000 women which included a lot of strippers. He would often pay them for their services.
  • He appeared as a contestant on Dancing with the Stars in 2019.
  • Over the years, he has appeared in several projects such as Entourage, Hood Of Horror, Fantasy Factory, Kobe Doin’ Work, Modern Family, Kourtney and Khloé Take Miami, Minute To Win It, etc.
  • In February 2021, Lamar agreed to fight against Aaron Carter in June 2021 in a celebrity boxing match. Lamar had won the boxing match in the second round.
