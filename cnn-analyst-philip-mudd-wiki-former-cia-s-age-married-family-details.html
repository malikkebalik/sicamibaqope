As professional as American counterterrorism and national security expert, Philip Mudd is in his career, the same he is when it comes to keeping his personal life away from public's glare with his humorous tweets.

The American-native appears on CNN as a counter-terrorism analyst. Besides, Philip is also the former Deputy Director of the Central Intelligence Agency (CIA)  Counterterrorist Center and the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI)’s National Security Branch.

Here is a list of interesting wikis about Philip Mudd and his family, education, career, and his marital status in 2018. 

1. Philip Mudd's Bio; Family, Education

The prominent analyst was born in 1962 in the United States in the family of Catholic Italian and Irish descent. His great-great-grandfather was Dr. Samuel A. Mudd, who set the broken leg of then-actor John Wilkes Booth after John assassinated Abraham Lincoln and jumped to the stage of Ford’s Theater on April 14, 1865.

Philip grew along with his brother in Pennsylvania. He graduated cum laude from the Villanova University with a Bachelor of Arts in English Literature in 1983. Later, he went to the University of Virginia, where he earned a master’s degree in English literature at the age of 22 in 1984. 

2. He Started Government Service At Age 23 From CIA 

Philip joined CIA in 1985 as an analyst, focusing on South Asia and the Middle East. In 1992, he started working in the CIA's Counterterrorist Center and later served as the Deputy National Intelligence Officer for Near East and South Asia to the National Intelligence Council from 1995 to 1998.

During the time, he began a policy assignment at the White House in early 2001 where he served as the Director for Gulf Affairs on the White House National Security Council.

After the September 11 attack, he went for a short assignment as the CIA member of the small diplomatic team that helped piece together a new government for Afghanistan, and he returned to CIA in early 2002.

In the establishment year of the FBI's National Security Branch in 2005, Philip was appointed by then-FBI Director Robert Mueller as the Branch's first-ever deputy director. He later became the FBI's Senior Intelligence Adviser and served until his resignment in 2010.

3. His Post Government Career On CNN, Published Two Books

Philip appears on the CNN network as a counterterrorism analyst. He assesses the motivations and intentions of foreign terrorist groups and their state and non-state sponsors.

As per wiki sites, Philip has also penned two books that comprise his analysis and viewpoint regarding the step against the terrorism.

Philip also owns a company called Mudd Management, which specializes in security consulting, analysis training and public speaking about security issues. Similarly, he serves as the Director of Enterprise Risk at SouthernSun Asset Management in Memphis, Tennessee. The company prides itself on identifying high-value companies and smart investments.
