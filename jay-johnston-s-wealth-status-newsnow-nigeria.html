Jay Johnston’s Biography

Born on 22 October 1968 in Chicago, Illinois USA, Jay Johnston is recognized as a comedian and actor, renowned for his involvement in the HBO comedy sketch series “Mr. Show” as a cast member and writer. He also played Officer Jay McPherson in “The Sarah Silverman Program”. His career in the entertainment industry, which started in the 1990s, has significantly contributed to his current net worth.

His lucrative career in comedy and acting, including numerous film appearances, has led to an estimated net worth of $4 million as of late-2017. With his continued efforts, it is expected that his wealth will keep growing.

Jay Johnston Net Worth $4 million

Johnston attended Columbia College Chicago, where he studied acting and comedy and eventually became part of the alumni that later joined “Mr. Show”. After graduation, he joined the touring comedy group Second City for several years, during which he participated in stage shows. His debut project, “Mr. Show with Bob and David”, a comedy sketch series, showcased his talent and contributed to the creation of numerous memorable characters and sketches. Johnston’s involvement in various TV programs and films further bolstered his net worth.

Post-“Mr. Show”, he made guest appearances on TV shows such as “Arrested Development”, “Parks and Recreation” and “Curb Your Enthusiasm”. Additionally, he appeared in movies like “Tenacious D in the Pick of Destiny” and “Anchorman: The Legend of Ron Burgundy”.

In 2006, Johnston became a regular in the show “American Misfits”, followed by his role in “The Sarah Silverman Program”. His career continued with the second series of “Nick Swardson’s Pretend Time”. Furthermore, he worked on the stop-motion animated series “Moral Orel” and voiced for “Mary Shelley’s Frankenhole” and the animated series “Bob’s Burgers”.

Regarding his personal life, Johnston was previously in a relationship with actress Paget Brewster, though details about his current relationship status remain undisclosed.
