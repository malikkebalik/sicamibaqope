How did Kid Rock start his Professional Career?

Regarding his professional career, Kid Rock was given an album deal by Jive Records after they saw him perform brilliantly as a part of the hip-hop group Boogie Down Productions.

With the release of his first album, “Grits Sandwiches for Breakfast,” he made his début in 1990. He then moved to Brooklyn and started working for Continuum Records after that.

In 1993, he issued his début album, “The Polyfuze Method.” The album received positive reviews for its unique musical style, but it received negative reviews for the song “Balls In Your Mouth.” In 1996, he released his album named “Early Morning Stoned Pimp” on his own label, Top Dog Records.

He also started his own band, Twisted Brown Trucker, at the same time. His fourth album, Devil Without a Cause, was then published in 1998 after he joined forces with Atlantic Records. His fourth record sold over 14 million copies after the hit single “Bawitdaba,” which was his breakthrough song.

Additionally, he released the road ballad-like song “Only God Knows Why,” which merits special mention for its representation. The following year, he made an appearance in Miami on “MTV Fashionably Loud.” Later, he published his second single video from the album “Devil Without a Cause,” “Bawitdaba,” with the aid of MTV.

Similar to this, his song “Cowboy” reached the Top 40 after being released and became an instant hit. He also took part in the Summer Sanitarium Tour in 2000, which featured acts like Metallica, System of a Down, and Powerman 5000.

Live Trucker, his debut live album, was published in 2006. He co-wrote “Rock ‘n’ Roll Never Forgets” with Bob Seger, which was published the following year. Additionally, he made a brief appearance in the movie “Larry the Cable Guy: Health Inspector.”

Born Free, one of his most popular albums, was published by him in 2010. He published his album “Care” that year, which was a protest against American politics. He published his most recent album, “Rebel Soul,” in 2012. In 2013, he participated in the “Best Night Ever” concert.
