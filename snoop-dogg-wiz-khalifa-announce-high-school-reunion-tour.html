Snoop Dogg &amp; Wiz Khalifa Announce High School Reunion Tour

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Monday, August 5, 2024

Snoop Dogg and Wiz Khalifa are joining forces for a star-studded North American trek this summer branded as the High School Reunion Tour.

The Mac & Devin Go to High School collaborators will be joined by Too $hort, Warren G, Berner, DJ Drama and more on the 33-city tour which will kick off North of the border in Vancouver on July 7.

AD LOADING...

Citi pre-sale tickets are available on Tuesday (March 7), while general admission tickets go on sale on Ticketmaster on March 10 at 9 a.m. local time.

Look for the tour to make stops in Utha, Denver, St. Louis, Brooklyn, Virginia Beach, Atlanta, Tampa Bay, New Orleans, Houston, Dallas, Sacramento, Phoenix and wrap up with a show in Irvine, California on August 27.

News of the tour comes after Wiz and Snoop teased what some thought was a sequel to 2012’s Mac & Devin Go To High School. However, it looks like it might have been teasing their newly announced tour.

“High school reunion comin summer 23 @wizkhalifa,” Snoop wrote in his post accompanied by a photo of him and Wiz in the studio together.

AD LOADING...

In his own Instagram post of the photo, the Pittsburgh native declared him and Snoop “the new Cheech and Chong” while using the hashtag “#highschoolreuinion.”

Last week, the pair linked up for lonely stoner anthem “Don’t Text Don’t Call.”

AD LOADING...

Prior to the High School Reunion Tour, Wiz will hit hit the road for a sprint through the Midwest as part of his seven-city The Good Trip Tour alongside Joey Bada$$, Berner, Smoke DZA and Chevy Woods.

That trek kicks off on April 15 in Kalamazoo, Michigan and includes stops in Milwaukee, Minneapolis, Lincoln and finishes up at Colorado’s famous Red Rocks Amphitheater on April 22.

Snoop Dogg and Wiz Khalifa also hinted at a sequel to 2012’s Mac & Devin Go To High School in the works.

Wiz Khalifa reflected on his friendship with Snoop Dogg in 2018 by showing love to Tha Doggfather, who he hailed as a “mentor” figure.

AD LOADING...

“Snoop is my homie, my homie, my mentor, my partner, my brother. It’s always good to have him involved and coaching me on,” Wiz told Build.

“The main thing he always does is just make sure that I’m being myself. He’s like, ‘Wiz, you so awesome, you so cool, people love you so much. You don’t have to be like nobody. You don’t gotta be like Snoop Dogg, you don’t gotta be like this person, just always be Wiz.’ That’s the most important thing he always tells me.”
