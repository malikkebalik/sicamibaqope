Payton took up the role of the group’s hostile facilitator the next year, helping the Mammoths win Super Bowl XXXV.

With Payton’s arrival, the squad recaptured its former glory and started winning games once more after struggling and rarely winning any games before his entrance (after a poor first season). The Saints coaching staff ran a bounty program between 2009 and 2011 that offered money to New Orleans defensive players who hurt opponents, according to information the NFL released in March 2012. After that turbulent off-season, the Saints finished 7-9 in 2012 but bounced back the following year with Payton back in charge, winning 11 games and securing a playoff spot.
