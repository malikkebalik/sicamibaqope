What Happens If You Vomit During Colonoscopy Prep

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Sunday, August 4, 2024

Are you scheduled for a colonoscopy? It's important to understand the preparation process and potential complications that may arise. One common concern is what happens if you vomit during colonoscopy prep. In this blog post, we will explore the consequences and complications of vomiting during colonoscopy prep and how Statcare can help you have a successful and stress-free colonoscopy.

Consequences of Vomiting During Colonoscopy Prep

Vomiting during colonoscopy prep can have several consequences, including:

  • Incomplete bowel preparation: Vomiting can disrupt the effectiveness of the bowel preparation process, leading to inadequate cleansing of the colon. This can result in a suboptimal colonoscopy and the need for a repeat procedure.

  • Dehydration: Vomiting can cause dehydration, especially if you are unable to keep fluids down. Dehydration can lead to dizziness, weakness, and electrolyte imbalances.

  • Delayed procedure: If you vomit during colonoscopy prep, your procedure may need to be rescheduled. This can be inconvenient and may prolong the time until any potential health issues are identified and addressed.

Complications of Vomiting During Colonoscopy Prep

In addition to the consequences mentioned above, vomiting during colonoscopy prep can also lead to complications such as:

  • Aspiration: If you vomit while lying down, there is a risk of aspirating the vomit into your lungs. This can cause respiratory problems and potentially lead to pneumonia.

  • Irritation of the esophagus: Frequent vomiting can irritate the lining of the esophagus, causing discomfort and potential damage.

  • Increased anxiety and discomfort: Vomiting during colonoscopy prep can be distressing and may increase anxiety and discomfort associated with the procedure.
