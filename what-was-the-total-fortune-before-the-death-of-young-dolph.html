Early Life

Adolph Thornton, Jr. was born in Chicago, Illinois on August 11, 1985. When he was two years old, his family relocated to Memphis, Tennessee. He has two brothers and two sisters.

He rapped in his smash song “Preach,” “Mama constantly in the streets, so guess who reared me?” In his music, he describes a time when his parents were crack addicts.

Career, Net Worth

Dolph has always had a passion for music. When his grandmother died in 2008, he was devastated. He turned to music, rapping, to communicate his thoughts and feelings.

The mixtape’s success pushed him to go deeper into the realm of music. He released several other mixtapes and singles over the years, including ‘High-Class Street Music,’ ‘A Time 2 Kill,’ ‘Blue Magic,’ ‘South Memphis Kingpin,’ and ‘Felix Brothers.’

In 2015, he was featured in O.T. Genasis’ smash track “Cut It,” which peaked at number 35 on the Billboard Hot 100. ‘King of Memphis,’ his debut album, was published in 2016. It was well-received by critics. It was also a commercial success, peaking at 49th on the Billboard 200 in the United States.

In April 2017, he released his next album, ‘Bulletproof,’ which peaked at number 36 on the US Billboard 200. It featured tunes like ‘100 Shots,’ ‘But I’m Bulletproof,’ and ‘I Pray for My Enemies.’

He released two more albums that year, ‘Tracking Numbers’ and ‘Thinking Out Loud.’ The former did not chart, whilst the latter peaked at number 16 on the US Billboard 200.
